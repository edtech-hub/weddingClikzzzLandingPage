How to Choose the Right Wedding Photographer in London

20 February 2026 ■ 5 min read ■ By Heston Photo
Home› Blog› Choosing a Wedding Photographer

Your wedding photographs are the one thing that will outlast the flowers, the cake, and even the memories that inevitably fade over time. Choosing the right photographer is one of the most important decisions you will make in the entire planning process — and in a city as large and competitive as London, it can feel overwhelming.

After years of photographing weddings across the UK, we've spoken to hundreds of couples about what they wish they'd known before booking. Here are the seven questions that matter most.

The Seven Questions to Ask

  • 1
    Can I see a full wedding gallery, not just highlights?
    Social media portfolios show the best ten images from a hundred weddings. A full gallery shows you how a photographer performs across an entire day — the ceremony, the reception, the family groups, and the quieter moments in between.
  • 2
    Have you photographed at my venue before?
    Familiarity with a venue — its light at different times of day, its best hidden corners, its logistical quirks — can make a significant difference to the quality of your images.
  • 3
    Have you photographed my type of wedding ceremony?
    Indian, Asian, and multicultural weddings have unique rituals, traditions, and timings. A photographer experienced in Hindu, Sikh, or Muslim ceremonies will know when to be ready for key moments — and when to step back respectfully.
  • 4
    What is your backup plan if something goes wrong?
    Camera failures happen. Ask whether your photographer shoots with two camera bodies, whether they back up images on the day, and whether they have a trusted colleague who could step in if they were ever incapacitated.
  • 5
    What does the contract cover?
    Ensure the contract specifies: number of hours covered, number of edited images delivered, delivery timeline, usage rights, and what happens if either party needs to cancel.
  • 6
    How long after the wedding will I receive my photos?
    Industry standard is between four and twelve weeks. Be wary of photographers who promise much faster turnaround times — rushed edits rarely produce the same quality as carefully considered post-processing.
  • 7
    Do I actually enjoy spending time with this person?
    Your photographer will be with you for eight to twelve hours on one of the most emotionally charged days of your life. Chemistry matters. If a consultation feels awkward, the wedding day will too.

"The right photographer doesn't just document your wedding — they become a quiet, trusted presence at the heart of it."

A Note on Price

Wedding photography in London ranges from a few hundred pounds to several thousand. While budget is a reality for every couple, we would gently caution against treating price as the primary filter. Photography is the only service from your wedding day that you will use every day for the rest of your life. A beautiful dress is worn once. A stunning venue is visited once. But your photographs will be on your walls, in your albums, and shown to your children and grandchildren for decades.

Invest in a photographer whose work moves you — and trust that the price reflects the skill, time, and experience behind it.
