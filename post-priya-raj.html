Priya & Raj's Golden Hindu Wedding at Syon House

12 March 2026 ■ 8 min read ■ By Heston Photo
Home› Blog› Priya & Raj at Syon House

When Priya and Raj first reached out to us, Priya's message simply said: "We want our wedding to feel like the pages of a fairytale." Standing in the Great Conservatory at Syon House on their wedding day, surrounded by golden candlelight, clouds of marigold petals, and the quiet joy of two families coming together, we knew we had every ingredient to make that wish come true.

The Mehendi Evening

The celebrations began the evening before with a Mehendi held in the family home in Ruislip. Priya sat cross-legged on cushions as the artist traced intricate patterns up her arms, her sisters gathered around her laughing and singing. The light in that room was impossibly warm — the kind you chase all day at a wedding and sometimes never find.

We photographed the Mehendi with a mix of documentary and intimate portrait work, letting the emotion of the moment speak for itself rather than orchestrating it. The result was a set of images Priya later described as her favourites from the entire two days.

"We barely noticed Heston Photo in the room. The photos looked like moments stolen from a dream — we genuinely forgot we were being photographed." — Priya

The Baraat

Raj arrived on horseback — a magnificent white stallion draped in flowers — flanked by his closest friends and family dancing to a dhol that echoed off the stone walls of Syon's forecourt. The energy was electric. We positioned ourselves both with the Baraat and ahead of it, capturing the procession from every angle without interrupting the flow of the celebration.

Syon House provides a magnificent backdrop with its neoclassical architecture and sweeping lawns. The cream stonework glowed against the deep blue April sky, giving every frame an almost cinematic quality.

The Mandap Ceremony

The Mandap had been decorated by Priya's aunt — a labour of love that took three days. Thousands of pale pink and ivory roses cascaded from a central canopy, and the air was heavy with the scent of jasmine and sandalwood incense. Raj's expression when Priya walked towards him, her lehenga a blaze of deep ruby red, is something we will never forget.

We worked with two cameras during the ceremony — one capturing the wider ritual moments, the other entirely focused on expressions and emotion. The Saat Phere, the seven circles around the sacred fire, produced some of the most moving photographs of the entire day.

The Vidaai

Nothing prepares you for the Vidaai. Even as photographers who have witnessed dozens, the moment a daughter leaves her family home — even in the most joyous of circumstances — carries a weight that is universal. Priya's mother held her for a long time before letting go. The photograph we took in that moment is one of the most powerful images in our portfolio.

As the car pulled away and the family waved, confetti cannons fired trails of gold petals into the evening air. It was the perfect punctuation mark on a perfect day.

"Thank you, Heston Photo, for seeing every part of our day — even the parts we didn't realise were happening. You gave us our wedding back." — Raj
